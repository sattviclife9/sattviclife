Padabhyanga with Kansa Thali

Kansa Thali is made from a special bronze alloy of copper, tin, and zinc. This metal interacts with the skin’s natural acidity (Pitta), and the grey residue that appears during the massage is not dirt but a sign of neutralization of excess acidity, helping to balance energy and heat in the body.

During Kansa Thali Padabhyanga, warm oils—often herbal or medicated—are used along with a Kansa bowl or wand to massage the soles of the feet and stimulate important marma (pressure) points. This therapy combines the nourishing benefits of Abhyanga (oil massage) with the unique therapeutic properties of Kansa metal, promoting deep relaxation, improved circulation, dosha balance, and overall healing.

It helps reduce dryness, pain, burning sensations in the soles, and stiffness in the feet and legs, while relieving foot tension and inducing overall relaxation. The therapy calms the mind, supports deep and restful sleep, and according to Ayurveda, nourishes vision, reduces and prevents sciatica pain and eye health. Additionally, it enhances blood circulation to the feet, creates a feeling of lightness, and keeps the soles soft, supple, and healthy.
