Swedan (Medicated Steam Bath)

Swedan is an Ayurvedic therapy that induces gentle sweating using medicated steam or warmth. It is commonly performed after Abhyanga (oil therapy) to enhance its effects.

It can be performed on the entire body or locally on specific areas such as the knee, neck, back, hands, and legs, as advised by the doctor and according to the disease condition.

Swedan helps by dilating body channels (Srotas), liquefying toxins (Ama) for easy elimination, and reducing stiffness, heaviness, and coldness in the body.

Swedan relieves joint pain, muscle stiffness, and swelling; balances Vata and Kapha dosha; improves blood circulation; delays aging; reduces body heaviness; enhances flexibility and mobility; supports detoxification; improves the effectiveness of Panchakarma therapies; improves vision; reduces tiredness and fatigue; nourishes body tissues (Dhatus); promotes longevity; induces sound sleep; and enhances skin tone and complexion.

Swedan is especially useful in conditions such as arthritis and joint disorders, back pain, neck pain, frozen shoulder, muscle spasm and stiffness, cold-related disorders, and as a post-Abhyanga therapy to open body channels.
